# Student-Depression-Analysis-Dashboard-Tableau-
Identified key behavioral patterns affecting mental health using data visualization, enabling actionable insights for early intervention.

# Project Overview
This project analyzes student mental health using data extracted from SQL and visualized in Tableau. The goal is to identify how lifestyle, academic pressure, and financial stress contribute to student depression.

# Tech Stack
SQL – Data extraction
Tableau – Dashboard & visualization
Data Analysis – Insight generation

# Key Insights (Data-Driven)
😴 Sleep Impact on Students
Majority of students (≈39%) sleep only 5–6 hours, indicating poor sleep habits.
Only ≈11% students get more than 8 hours of sleep.
Lower sleep duration is strongly linked with higher stress levels.

📚 Study Hours Trend
Study activity peaks around mid-range hours (6–8 hrs), with student count reaching ~42–45 students.
Indicates most students operate under moderate to high workload.

😊 Study Satisfaction
Highest satisfaction level (SS:4) includes ~28% of students (13 out of 46).
Very low satisfaction (SS:1 & SS:2) still accounts for ~37%, showing a significant dissatisfaction segment.

📉 Academic Pressure
Highest academic pressure (AP:5) is experienced by ~28% students (13 students).
Moderate pressure levels (AP:3 & AP:1) also contribute significantly (~39% combined).

💰 Financial Stress
High financial stress levels (FS:5) affect ~22% of students.
Moderate stress (FS:3) is most common at ~28%, indicating financial concerns are widespread.

# Key Takeaways
 
A large portion of students suffer from poor sleep and high academic pressure, both major contributors to mental health issues.
Financial stress is not extreme but consistently present across students.
There is a clear imbalance between effort (study hours) and satisfaction, indicating burnout risk.
